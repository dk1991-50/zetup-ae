# Top 10 Business Activities for Dubai Mainland Company Setup

**Answer Capsule:**
The most popular business activities for Dubai mainland companies are general trading, management consultancy, IT services, marketing consultancy, real estate brokerage, restaurant/cafe operation, construction contracting, recruitment services, educational training, and e-commerce trading. Each activity has specific licence requirements, government fees, and approval processes through DET and relevant secondary authorities.

Choosing the right business activity — or combination of activities — determines your licence type, fee structure, and which government approvals you need. DET offers over 2,000 activity codes, and selecting the wrong ones can lead to costly rejections or unnecessary approvals.

## The Top 10 Activities

**1. General Trading**
The broadest commercial activity, allowing import, export, and wholesale/retail trade in a wide range of goods. Requires a commercial licence. Popular because it provides maximum trading flexibility. Note: general trading licences cost more than single-product trading licences.

**2. Management Consultancy**
One of the most popular professional activities for expat entrepreneurs. Covers business consulting, strategy advisory, and operational management services. Requires a professional licence. No additional department approvals for standard consultancy.

**3. Information Technology Services**
Covers software development, IT support, systems integration, and technology consulting. Professional licence. High demand sector in Dubai's growing tech ecosystem.

**4. Marketing Consultancy**
Covers digital marketing, advertising consultancy, PR, and media planning. Professional licence. Often combined with management consultancy for broader scope.

**5. Real Estate Brokerage**
Requires RERA (Real Estate Regulatory Agency) registration and a broker qualification. Commercial licence. One of the highest-revenue activities in Dubai but comes with additional regulatory requirements.

**6. Restaurant / Cafe Operation**
Requires Dubai Municipality food safety approval, kitchen layout plans, and potentially DHA health permits. Commercial licence. Complex approval process but large market.

**7. Construction Contracting**
Requires Dubai Municipality contractor classification. Commercial or industrial licence depending on scope. New contractor licensing under Law No. 7 of 2025 (effective January 2026) requires registration in the Central Contractor Register.

**8. Recruitment Services**
Placing employees with client companies. Requires MOHRE approval. Professional licence. Growing demand due to Emiratisation requirements driving Emirati recruitment activity.

**9. Educational Training**
Covers corporate training, professional development, tutoring, and educational consulting. Requires KHDA approval. Professional licence.

**10. E-Commerce Trading**
Online retail and wholesale. Can be added to existing commercial licences. Dubai's e-commerce sector has grown substantially, supported by government digital economy initiatives.

## Multi-Activity Licensing

You can register multiple activities on a single mainland licence. Additional activities cost approximately AED 1,000–2,000 each in DET fees. Common combinations: management consultancy + marketing consultancy + IT services (covering a broad professional services scope), or general trading + e-commerce (covering both physical and online retail).

## Choosing the Right Activities

Match activities to what your business actually does — not what sounds broad or impressive. Overly broad activity selections can trigger unnecessary approvals from secondary departments and increase fees. Too narrow, and you may need to amend your licence later when your services expand.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I change my business activities after formation?**
A: Yes. Activity amendments are processed through DET at approximately AED 1,000–2,000 per change. Some changes may require additional approvals.

**Q: How many activities can I have on one licence?**
A: There is no hard limit, but each additional activity increases fees. Most companies register 2–5 activities.

**Q: Do different activities have different licence fees?**
A: Yes. Commercial licences are generally more expensive than professional licences. Activities requiring secondary department approvals add cost and time.

**Q: What is the difference between a commercial and professional licence?**
A: A commercial licence allows trading in goods (buying, selling, import/export). A professional licence covers service-based activities (consulting, technology, education). Some businesses need both.

**Q: Can I do general trading from a flexi-desk office?**
A: DET allows general trading from a flexi-desk for the initial licence period. However, if you plan to store inventory or receive goods, you will need warehouse space.
