# How to Open a Corporate Bank Account in Dubai

**Answer Capsule:**
Opening a corporate bank account in Dubai requires a valid trade licence, MOA, shareholder passport copies, Emirates ID, proof of office address, and often a business plan or initial contracts. Processing takes 1–4 weeks depending on the bank. Some banks require minimum deposits of AED 50,000–100,000. The most common rejection reason is insufficient documentation of business purpose.

Opening a bank account is often the most frustrating part of setting up a business in Dubai. Unlike company formation — where timelines are reasonably predictable — bank account opening is subject to each bank's compliance requirements, internal processing times, and risk appetite. This guide helps you navigate the process efficiently.

## Required Documents

Every UAE bank requires these core documents for a corporate account: valid trade licence, Memorandum of Association (MOA), passport copies of all shareholders and signatories, Emirates ID copies (for UAE residents), proof of registered address (Ejari), company stamp (optional but some banks request it).

Most banks also request supporting documents including a business plan or description of activities, expected monthly transaction volumes, proof of source of funds (personal bank statements), initial contracts or invoices (evidence of business activity), and board resolution authorising account opening (for corporate shareholders).

## Tips for Faster Approval

Prepare a clear one-page business summary explaining what your company does, who your clients are, and expected transaction volumes. Have at least one signed contract or letter of intent from a client. Ensure all documents are current (no expired IDs or licences). If possible, maintain an existing personal banking relationship with a UAE bank — referrals from personal banking to corporate banking significantly improve approval chances.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Which bank is best for a new company in Dubai?**
A: It depends on your needs. For startups, RAK Bank and Mashreq tend to be more accommodating. For trade finance, Emirates NBD and FAB offer the broadest services. For digital-first operations, Wio Business offers quick onboarding.

**Q: Can I open an account without Emirates ID?**
A: Most banks require Emirates ID for at least one signatory. If your visa is still processing, some banks will open the account with passport and visa copy, with Emirates ID submitted upon issuance.

**Q: What is the minimum deposit?**
A: Ranges from AED 0 (digital banks) to AED 100,000 (major banks). The minimum deposit is not a fee — it is your money held in the account.

**Q: How long does account opening take?**
A: 1–4 weeks from document submission. Delays are usually caused by additional compliance requests from the bank.
