# What Is a PRO Service in Dubai? Everything Expats Need to Know

**Answer Capsule:**
A PRO (Public Relations Officer) service in Dubai is a professional government liaison company that handles all interactions between your business and UAE government agencies — including visa applications, trade licence renewals, Emirates ID processing, labour cards, and document attestation with MOHRE, DET, and GDRFA. Most Dubai companies with 5+ employees outsource this function to save time, reduce errors, and avoid navigating Arabic-language government procedures.

If you have recently set up a company in Dubai or are planning to, you have probably heard the term "PRO services" mentioned frequently. For European and Western expat entrepreneurs, the concept can be confusing — it does not exist in most other countries. This post explains exactly what PRO services are, when you need them, and how to avoid the common pitfalls.

## Why PRO Services Exist in Dubai

The UAE's government infrastructure involves multiple separate agencies that a business must interact with regularly. Unlike many Western countries where company registration, tax, immigration, and labour are handled through unified digital portals, the UAE still requires interaction with distinct entities — DET for licensing, MOHRE for labour, GDRFA for immigration, ICP for identity documents, DHA for medical testing, and various others.

Each agency has its own procedures, document requirements, and processing timelines. Many processes require physical visits to government service centres. Forms and systems are primarily in Arabic. And requirements change frequently — sometimes without formal announcement.

A PRO company employs trained professionals who navigate this system on your behalf. They know what each department needs, they have established relationships with government officials, and they handle the logistics so you do not have to spend your working days in government queues.

## What Does a PRO Company Actually Do?

In practical terms, your PRO company handles visa processing for every employee you hire (and their families), annual trade licence renewal with DET, establishment card and immigration card renewals, Emirates ID applications and renewals, medical fitness test coordination, document attestation and legalisation, Ejari registration, labour card processing, and increasingly, Emiratisation compliance management.

For a company with 30 employees, this translates to approximately 100–200 individual government transactions per year — each requiring specific documents, fees, and follow-up.

## When Do You Need PRO Services?

**From day one if you are forming a company.** Your formation consultant may handle the initial setup, but ongoing PRO needs begin immediately — processing your investor visa, setting up establishment and immigration cards, and handling your first employee visas.

**Definitely when you have 10+ employees.** At this point, the volume of visa renewals, ID processing, and compliance obligations makes self-handling impractical.

**Urgently if you face Emiratisation requirements.** Companies with 20+ employees in designated sectors or 50+ employees in any sector need a PRO partner with Emiratisation expertise.

## How Much Should You Pay?

The market ranges from AED 1,500/month for basic packages (small companies) to AED 35,000/month for comprehensive corporate retainers. For a company with 25–50 employees, expect to pay AED 8,000–15,000/month for quality service.

Key cost principle: your PRO retainer covers the company's service fee. Government fees (visa processing, licence renewal, Emirates ID) are separate and should be passed through at cost with receipts. If a provider blends these together, you cannot verify what the government actually charges versus what the company is adding as margin.

## How to Evaluate a PRO Company

Five questions every expat entrepreneur should ask:

1. **Can you send me an itemised quote?** The quote should separate your service fee from government fees, with no vague "processing charges."
2. **How quickly do you respond to WhatsApp messages?** Test this during evaluation. If they are slow now, they will be slower later.
3. **Can I visit your office?** A physical office in a real building is your basic verification against scam operators.
4. **What is your termination clause?** Anything longer than 30–60 days protects them, not you.
5. **Who will be my day-to-day contact?** You want a named person, not a generic support line.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do I legally need a PRO company?**
A: Not legally, but practically, yes. Any company with employees needs to process visas, renew licences, and interact with government agencies regularly. Most companies outsource this.

**Q: Can I use a typing centre instead of a PRO company?**
A: Typing centres handle individual transactions. A PRO company provides ongoing account management, compliance tracking, and proactive deadline management. For 10+ employees, a PRO company's retainer model is significantly more efficient.

**Q: What if I speak Arabic — do I still need PRO services?**
A: Language is only part of the equation. PRO expertise means knowing procedures, document requirements, and processing nuances that change frequently. Even Arabic-speaking business owners typically outsource PRO for efficiency.

**Q: How do I switch from my current PRO to ZETUP?**

**Q: Are PRO services tax deductible?**
A: Yes. PRO retainer fees are deductible business expenses for UAE Corporate Tax purposes.

**Q: What is the difference between a PRO company and a business setup consultant?**
A: Business setup consultants handle company formation (a one-time project). PRO companies handle ongoing government liaison after formation. Some companies offer both, but they are distinct services.
